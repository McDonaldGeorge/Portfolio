Project Overview

The Monsters Learn To Fight Back is a Unity-based combat simulation where monsters learn to fight using reinforcement learning. Built using C#, custom movement logic, and training systems inspired by Dungeons & Dragons combat rules, this project demonstrates adaptive AI behaviour, environment design, and machine learning integration.


Initially developed during my final year at university in 2024, this project demonstrated early success in applying AI to turn-based monster behaviour, but also revealed key challenges and areas for improvement. The work is ongoing, with future updates planned to incorporate NEAT-driven evolutionary learning, refined strategy evaluation, and multi-agent coordination to create richer, more adaptive combat scenarios.

The Problem

Since its debut in 1974, Dungeons & Dragons (D&D) has remained a cornerstone of strategic, turn-based combat and collaborative storytelling. However, as encounters grow larger and more complex, the cognitive load placed on Dungeon Masters (DMs) increases significantly. Coordinating multiple monsters, their tactics, and optimal decision-making can slow down gameplay and disrupt the narrative flow.


The Monsters Learn to Fight Back explores how artificial intelligence can enhance monster behaviour by allowing enemies to develop their own strategies through adaptive algorithms. By integrating learning-based AI—such as evolutionary methods and reinforcement learning techniques—this project aims to create monsters that act more intelligently and dynamically in combat. The ultimate goal is to reduce DM workload while producing more engaging, reactive, and believable encounters for players.

My Approach

  • Designed a grid-based combat environment in Unity
  • Implemented movement, adjacency rules, and attack mechanics in C#
  • Created an environment API flexible enough to support:
    • Unity ML-Agents (PPO)
    • Custom NEAT implementation (in-progress)
  • Structured training episodes with incremental reward shaping
  • Logged behaviour and performance across training iterations

Results

The AI learns to:

  • Move intelligently toward the player
  • Stop in optimal attack range
  • Attack without human intervention
  • Evaluate success/failure based on environment events
  • Evolve strategies over time

Future updates will include NEAT-based evolutionary learning, multi-agent coordination, and more advanced DnD-style tactical behaviour.
